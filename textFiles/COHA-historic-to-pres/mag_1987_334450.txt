The statistics are nothing to celebrate : 58% of high school seniors admit to having tried illegal drugs at least once . But the percentage of students experimenting with illicit drugs actually dropped last year  --  as it has every year but one since 1978 . That encouraging news comes from the twelfth annual survey on teenage drug use conducted by the University of Michigan 's Institute for Social Research . <P> Illicit drug use by teenagers last year was 3% lower than in 1985 . The ! number of students using cocaine showed a modest overall decline , though frequency of use  --  especially of crack  --  has grown . For legal drugs , the numbers are more disturbing . Nearly two-thirds reported prior-month use of alcohol , and 19% admitted to being daily cigarette smokers  --  a small drop for the smokers and virtually no change for the drinkers .
